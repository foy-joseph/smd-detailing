Honesty section

What I don't do.

Most detailers list everything they can do. Here's the stuff I deliberately won't — because saying no to the wrong jobs is how I keep the standard up.

✕ I don't take cars I can't restore safely.

If it needs a body shop, smoke remediation or a respray, I'll tell you straight. I'll even recommend the right place. That's not weakness — that's the right call.

✕ I don't use sponges.

Sponges hold grit. Grit swirls your paint. I use a proper two-bucket method with grit guards and microfibre mitts. Every single wash.

✕ I don't rush.

The Concours takes 8–14 hours because it takes 8–14 hours. The price is the time. If a job's quoted at 6 hours, I won't hand it back at 4.

✕ I don't take more than 5–8 cars a week.

That's the cap during the school year. It's how I make sure every car gets the same attention as the last. Hence the waitlist.

✕ I don't quote without seeing the car.

The prices are fixed for normal-condition cars. If a vehicle is unusually filthy, oversized or has special-condition paintwork, I'll give you a supplement upfront — never a surprise at pickup.

✕ I don't discount.

The price is the price. If The Service is too much, The Weekly is €70 and still ten times what a sponge gets you. I'd rather earn your trust on a Mini than cut corners on a Full.
